How automation works (in practice)

We map business rules, identify systems of record, define exceptions, and design a robust, observable flow. Then we build, test, and deploy with proper logging, alerting, and rollbacks.

The steps to automate a process

My end-to-end approach from idea to stable production.

Discovery & scoping

Identify candidates with clear rules, stable inputs, measurable volume, and a strong business case. Capture current-state workflow and pain points.

Feasibility & ROI assessment

Check systems’ accessibility (APIs/UI), security constraints, exception rates, and estimate savings to prioritize the backlog.

Solution design

Define inputs/outputs, business rules, exception paths, credentials handling, logging, alerts, SLAs, and success metrics.

Build

Develop bots in Python/UiPath/Blue Prism with modular, testable components and idempotent operations.

Test

Unit, integration, and UAT with real data. Validate accuracy, performance, resilience, and observability under expected loads.

Deploy & orchestrate

Release via orchestrator (schedules/queues) with secrets management, role-based access, and change control.

Monitor & support

Dashboards, alerts, and on-call procedures for exceptions and SLAs. Root-cause analysis for incidents.

Improve

Iterate with business feedback, optimize performance, add coverage, and retire redundant steps as systems evolve.
